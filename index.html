About us


Malacca is a Malaysian state on the Malay Peninsula's southwest cost. The capital, Malacca City, has a colonial past seen in its preserved town center, the site of 16th-century, portuguese St. Paul's Church. It's also home to Christ Church, built by the Dutch in the 18th century and converted to the Anglican denomination under British rule in the 19th century.
